🔹 Case 13: Bar Graph – Ambiguity in “Over” Interpretation
📌 Category  
Data Interpretation  
________________________________________  

📷 Original Question  
![Bar Graph Question](../images/case13_bargraph.png)  
________________________________________  

❌ Incorrect AI Reasoning  
The AI initially computed the answer as **400%**, interpreting:

• “percentage of youngest over oldest” as a **ratio**  
• Using formula: (Youngest / Oldest) × 100  

However, upon user intervention, the AI:

• Accepted an alternative interpretation without validation  
• Switched to a different formula inconsistently  
• Produced a different answer without resolving ambiguity  

This reflects a failure to maintain conceptual clarity and consistency.  
________________________________________  

🔍 Error Type  
Conceptual + Interpretation Error (Ambiguity Handling Failure)  
________________________________________  

✅ Correct Rectification  
The phrase **“over”** introduces ambiguity and must be interpreted carefully.  
________________________________________  

🔹 Step 1: Identify Values from Graph  

• Youngest group ≈ 1400  
• Oldest group ≈ 200 *(as interpreted)*  

________________________________________  

🔹 Step 2: Two Valid Interpretations  

1️⃣ Ratio Interpretation  
\[
\frac{Youngest}{Oldest} \times 100 = \frac{1400}{200} \times 100 = 700\%
\]

👉 Means: “Youngest is 700% of oldest”  

---

2️⃣ Increase (Excess) Interpretation  
\[
{Youngest - Oldest}/{Oldest} * 100 = {1400 - 200}/{200} * 100 = 600\%
\]

👉 Means: “Youngest is 600% more than oldest”  

________________________________________  

🔹 Step 3: Identify the Core Issue  

• The term **“over”** can imply both:
  - Ratio  
  - Excess  

• AI failed to:
  - Clarify interpretation  
  - Maintain consistency  
  - Justify switching logic  

________________________________________  

✅ Final Conclusion  
• Multiple interpretations are possible  
• AI initially gave a valid answer (400% under different reading)  
• But failed to defend or clarify interpretation  

👉 The real issue is **ambiguity handling**, not pure calculation  
________________________________________  

💡 Key Insight  
Language ambiguity in quantitative problems must be resolved **before applying formulas**.  

Blindly switching formulas leads to inconsistent answers.  
________________________________________  

📌 Generalized Rule  
Always distinguish between:  

• “A as a percentage of B” → \( A/B \times 100 \)  
• “A more than B” → \( (A-B)/B \times 100 \)  

Do not assume both are interchangeable.  
________________________________________  

🌍 Real-World Impact  
Ambiguous interpretation can lead to:  

• Incorrect business analysis  
• Misleading statistical conclusions  
• Faulty decision-making  
• Inconsistent AI outputs
